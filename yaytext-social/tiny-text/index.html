The Significance of Superscript and Subscript

Superscript and subscript text have a rich historical background, originating from the need to represent mathematical and scientific notations, footnotes, and linguistic elements:

  • Superscript: Text raised above the baseline, often used for exponents in equations (e.g., "x²").
  • Subscript: Text lowered below the baseline, commonly used for indices (e.g., "H₂O").
  • Footnotes often employ superscript numbers to reference additional information.

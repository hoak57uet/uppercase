1. What is ROT13?

ROT13 (Rotate by 13) is a simple substitution cipher used to encode text. Each letter is replaced by the letter 13 positions ahead in the alphabet. It is a special case of the Caesar cipher and is used primarily for obfuscation rather than secure encryption.

2. Why should I use ROT13?

ROT13 is used for simple encoding to obscure text or data. It is often employed in online forums and puzzles. While it does not provide strong security, it can help protect against casual browsing or accidental exposure.
